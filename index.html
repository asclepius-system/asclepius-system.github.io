Micro-Benchmark Results

(a) Different
ear canal
environment: 		We examine the impact of the ear canal environment on PCG signal quality. We divide the normal ear canal condition into two groups (dry and wet ear canal), and invite a volunteer to conduct the same experiment before and after a shower. As shown in the figure, we find the ear canal environment have negligible impact to Ascleipus, the average RMSE only degrade from 1.08% to 1.12%.
(b) Different
human activity
status: 		We then examine the impact of different human activity status, We invite a volunteer to conduct same experiment before and after a running workout (termed as "normal status" and "after running"). We find the running workout decrease the performance of Asclepius with the RMSE drops from 0.85% to 1.68%. One reason might be the workout involves significant interference noises (e.g., the vibrations of the chest) to heart sound auscultation. We thus suggest our users do not user Asclepius after a training workout directly.
(c) Different
human position: 		Next, we examine the impact of different human positions for auscultation, specifically, sitting, standing, and lying down. We find the sitting position have smaller average RMSE (0.8%) while involves higher variances. The reason might be the sitting position is more likely to cause vital motion noises. As for the other two positions, we find the average RMSE are similar (1.0% and 1.1%).
(d) Different
noise level: 		We then examine the impact of the ambient noise. We play a piece of sound in the lab with different noise levels (ranges from 45 to 60 dBSPL) while conducting the experiment. In the benchmark study, we find the RMSE increase slightly when noise level increases from 45 to 55dBSPL. One possible reason might be the closure of the earphone isolates the ambient noise from the outside sounds effectively.
(e) Different
video meeting
platforms: 		We finally assess the impact of different video meeting platforms on PCG quality. In the benchmark study, We tested four widely used video meeting platforms including Zoom, Skype, VooV and Webex. We select the high-fidelity mode on each platform and conduct the experiment. Our finding shows significant variation in performance across different platforms. The VooV meeting has worst performance with the average RMSE of 2.8%. followed by Zoom (2.4%) and Skype (2.4%). The Webex meeting has the best performance with the average RMSE of 1.7%, however, its performance is still worse compared to the previous study without video meeting. The reason might be the audio compression algorithm adopted by these video meeting platform negatively affected the audio quality. We leave it for future improvement.
